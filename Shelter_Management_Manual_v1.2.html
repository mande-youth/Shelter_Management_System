1. Introduction

This manual accompanies the Shelter Management Workbook v1.2, designed to support Lebanese Red Cross Youth teams in managing shelter operations efficiently and accurately.

What is this workbook for?

  • Tracking households in one specific shelter.
  • Managing room assignments and capacity.
  • Recording distributions and monitoring unmet needs.
  • Scheduling follow-ups for vulnerable cases.

Who uses this?

  • Youth Volunteers: Data entry and daily updates.
  • Shelter Focal Points: Room planning and distribution oversight.
  • Data/M&E Teams: Quality assurance and reporting.

✨ What's New in v1.2?

  • Focus Cell Enabling: Sheets now open directly at the correct working cell to reduce scrolling errors.
  • Automatic Room Summaries: The Room_Summary sheet is now fully derived (automatic), requiring no manual calculations.
  • Sector Origin Tracking: New fields to track which sector referred the household (EMS, DM, etc.) for better coordination.

3. Core Principles & Data Rules

To ensure reports are accurate, all users must follow these non-negotiable rules:

  • One Shelter Scope: This workbook is for a single shelter only.
  • Household-Based Model: One household = one row in HH_Master. Never create a second row for the same family.
  • One Primary Room: Assign each household to one primary room.
  • Blank vs. 0:
    • Blank = Unknown / Not yet verified.
    • 0 = Verified None (you asked and confirmed there are zero).
  • Needs Fields: These show current unmet needs ONLY. Do not log what has already been provided here.
  • Date Rule: Only Next_Follow_Up_Date may be in the future. All other dates (like Intake Date, Distribution Date) must be today or in the past.
  • Privacy: No medical diagnoses. Record operational needs only (e.g., "Needs insulin refill" not "Diabetic patient").
  • No Macros: This file contains no VBA/Macros. Do not add any.
⚠️ CRITICAL REASON Most common reason reports are wrong: Distributions are logged in the Log sheet, but the user forgets to reduce the "Unmet Need" in HH_Master. This results in double-counting the need.

5. Workflow 1: Intake & Registration

Use HH_Master to register a new household.

1

Search First: Check if the household already exists by searching Primary_Phone or Contact_Name.

Why? Prevents duplicate rows.

2

Create Record: If new, go to the first empty row. Enter a unique Household_ID.

3

Enter Demographics: Fill Head of Household name, total people, and age/gender breakdowns.

4

Assign Room: Enter the Assigned_Room (e.g., "R1", "Hall A"). This must match the room names used in Room_Summary exactly.

5

Enter Sector Origin (NEW):

Use the Sector_Origin field to indicate which sector referred or linked this household.

  • Values: Youth, EMS, DM, DRR, BTS, MSS, HQ.
  • Rule: Select when known. If unknown, leave blank (do not guess).
Screenshot: HH_Master – Location of Sector_Origin dropdown (Column E).
6

Save & Review: Check for any Red or Amber flags that appear immediately after entry.

6. Workflow 2: Needs & Follow-Up

Updating Unmet Needs

The Needs columns in HH_Master are "live" snapshots. They should be updated whenever the situation changes.

Categories explained:

  • Medical Need Flag: Does the person have a health condition requiring attention?
  • Need_Medicine_Flag: Specifically requires medication (distinct from general medical need).
  • Special Needs Flag: Non-medical specific needs (e.g., disability assistance, specific dietary requirements).
  • Other Unmet Need: Miscellaneous items not covered by standard columns.

Follow-Up Dates

The Next_Follow_Up_Date is the only field where a future date is allowed. This tells the team when to check on this household next.

⚠️ Troubleshooting Dates If you cannot enter a date, check if it is a future date. Future dates are blocked in all columns except Next_Follow_Up_Date.

7. Workflow 3: Distributions

This workflow requires two separate actions to keep data clean.

Part A: Logging the Distribution

Go to Distribution_Log.

1

Select the Household_ID (must match Master).

2

Enter Distribution_Date (Must be today or past).

3

Select Item and Quantity.

4

Fill Sector_Origin_Snapshot (NEW):

This captures the sector origin at the time of distribution for audit trails.

  • Values: Youth, EMS, DM, DRR, BTS, MSS, HQ.
  • Rule: Must be filled for each distribution row when known.
Screenshot: Distribution_Log – Sector_Origin_Snapshot column.

Part B: Reconciliation (Mandatory)

🔴 STOP - Do not skip this step If you only log the distribution but don't reduce unmet needs, the workbook will still show the household as needing the item.
1

Go back to HH_Master. Find the household row.

2

Reduce the value in the relevant Need_... column.

Example: If Need_Blankets was 2, and you distributed 2, change to 0.

3

Confirm totals make sense.

8. Room Planning & Room Summary (v1.2)

The Room_Summary sheet is automatic. It uses formulas to count people based on HH_Master.

ℹ️ Derived Sheet Do NOT edit totals cells. Everything is calculated automatically. You may only type Room Names in Column A to label the rooms.

How to use it:

  1. Go to Room_Summary.
  2. Type the Room Name in Column A (e.g., "Room 101", "Hall B").
    Tip: Ensure the name matches exactly what you typed in HH_Master.
  3. Review the totals (People, Males, Females, etc.).

Exited Households

Households with status "Exited" are automatically excluded from Room_Summary totals.

🚫 Common Error

If totals look wrong, check if:

  1. The Assigned_Room spelling in HH_Master matches the Room Name in Room_Summary exactly.
  2. The household status is not "Exited".

9. QA, Error Prevention & Common Mistakes

📋 Daily/Weekly QA Checklist

  • Check for missing Household_ID.
  • Check for missing Assigned_Room in active households.
  • Review Red/Amber flags and fix errors.
  • Verify recent distributions were reconciled (Needs reduced in Master).
  • Check Sector_Origin is filled for new intakes.

Common Mistakes & Fixes

Mistake Impact Fix
Typing into derived Room_Summary cells. Breaks formulas. Only type Room Names in Column A. Do not touch totals.
Forgetting reconciliation after distributions. Reports show false needs. Always follow the 2-step distribution workflow.
Using future dates in "Last Contacted". Data validation error. Only use future dates for Next_Follow_Up_Date.
Confusing Blank vs 0. Unknown vs. Verified None. Use 0 only if verified. Leave blank if not asked.
Inconsistent Room Naming (e.g., "R1" vs "Room 1"). Double counting or missing data in Room_Summary. Stick to a standard: "R1", "R2", "Family Area A".
Creating a new row for an existing household. Duplicate counts. Always search by Phone/Name first.
Deleting rows for exited households. Loss of historical data. Change status to "Exited". Do not delete row.
Entering diagnostic medical details. Privacy breach. Keep notes operational: "Needs medicine" is enough.

10. Troubleshooting & Support

"Room_Summary totals look wrong"

Check spelling consistency between HH_Master and Room_Summary. Check if household status is "Exited".

"A date is rejected"

You are trying to enter a future date in a past/present only field. Only Next_Follow_Up_Date allows future dates.

"Dropdowns don't appear"

The file might be in "Protected View". Click "Enable Editing" at the top of the spreadsheet software.

"Copy/paste broke the formatting"

Paste as "Values" only (Ctrl+Shift+V or Paste Special > Values). Avoid pasting cell colors or borders from other sheets.
